# tithe — DESIGN v2 (the translation)

This document designs the complete arc of tithe — acts 2 and 3 and the ending —
by translating the design anatomy of Universal Paperclips
(`docs/research/PAPERCLIPS-DESIGN.md`, cited below by law number, and
`docs/research/paperclips-interface.md`) into tithe's world, subject to tithe's
own laws. It extends the binding contract in `docs/DESIGN.md` and the family
laws in `CLAUDE.md`. It is written to be built from: every constant, cost,
reveal condition, and string below is the intended shipping value unless a
pace-sim run says otherwise.

Where this document conflicts with DESIGN.md §9 or CLAUDE.md, those win.

Act 1, turn 1, and the shipped taste of act 2 (priests, two miracles, the
teased tithe) are not redesigned here. Deviations from the act-2/3 sketch in
DESIGN.md §3 are flagged inline as `OWNER CALL`. Changes required to shipped
content are collected in §6.6.

---

## 1. the arc

UP ran 44% / 19% / 33% / 4% over ~7h45m. Tithe is a single evening, and its
heart is the middle act, not the first — act 1's desk-mastery is already
shipped and short by design.

OWNER CALL: total runtime — default one sitting, ~90 minutes attended
(bot 35–45 min, per DESIGN.md §4). Alternative: two sittings, ~150 minutes,
the save carrying across; everything below stretches by ×1.6.

| act | boundary | attended span | share | bot target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — the settlement | start → first offering (turn 1) | 0–15 min | ~17% | 0–6 min |
| 2 — the congregation | → ascension (molt 3) | 15–60 min | ~50% | 6–22 min |
| 3 — the hunger | → the last star | 60–85 min | ~28% | 22–34 min |
| the ending | the choice → the last gathering | 85–93 min | ~5% | 34–38 min |

Act 1 plays as honest survival (shipped). Act 2 inverts the economy: people
stop being the point and become the pipe — favor becomes the wallet at the
tithe (molt 2), and the act ends in a savings race for ascension. Act 3 snaps
scope: the field's stars become the world board, heralds replicate, the
silence presses, three skies are emptied. The ending is the player performing
the final molt by hand, one organ at a time, and then answering the flecks.

The Hunger Wall remains the only allowed >3 min flat stretch (CLAUDE.md).
Acts 2–3 pace by staggered ladders (Law 8): faith gates, arrivals, deeper
offerings, legend drip — three timers minimum, something always 30–90 s from
ringing. The one deliberate cliffhanger (Law 20) is the ascension race (§6.5).

## 2. currency genealogy

The deepest UP idea — molt, never inflate (Law 9) — under tithe's one-screen
law means transmutation in place, never demolition. Every number below is born
in a slot that already exists at boot (ghost or dark), lives one to three
regimes, and dies in place: blanked, frozen, or transmuted. Stranded balances
are never refunded or mentioned (Law 11) — except once, at the very end, when
the dead rows wake with single digits (§8).

| currency | born | earned how | spent on | dies |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| food | t+0, first click | gather → foragers → farms; surges | upkeep; arrivals (threshold) | molt 3 — the village is consumed; balance stranded. wakes once in §8 at 2.0 |
| wood | ~t+15 s | cut wood → woodcutters → sawpit | works, projects, the tithe's last bill | molt 2 — row blanks at whatever it held. wakes once in §8 at 3.0 |
| stone | with the quarry | masons | works, the temple, the tithe's last bill | molt 2 — same death as wood. wakes once in §8 at 5.0 |
| villagers → stock | <60 s | arrivals at food thresholds | offerings (1, named); deeper offerings (1/2/4/8); ascension (all) | molt 3 at exactly 0 — the only currency to die complete; it was the key (Law 19) |
| favor | first offering | offerings; priests; the tithe; reaped worlds | miracles, the temple, heralds, the vigil, the last dismantle | §8, billed at exactly the remaining balance, to the tenth (Law 23) |
| faith | turn 1, at 1 | favor gates (8) + deeper offerings (4) — ladder sums to 13 | nothing until ascension, which costs all 13 | molt 3 at 13/13 — complete, like UP trust at 100/100 |
| legend | first time favor sits at cap | only while favor is capped (Law 7), 0.3/s | the songs, a calendar, the count | molt 3 — stranded at whatever it held; the flock's stories die with the flock |
| doubt | first deeper offering | +1 per deeper offering; +1 per 90 s while any remain | not spent — an anti-currency; cured by `a sign` | molt 3 — the doubters are consumed with everyone else; no one mentions it |
| souls | first world tithed | world populations, on conversion | sky doors (2.0e12, 2.0e15) | the last star — final total frozen, the score that outlives the game |
| lights (worlds) | molt 3 — the 14 stars were always there | converted by heralds; dimmed by the silence | not spent — the board itself | the last star, at 42/42 across three skies |
| heralds | act 3 | seeded with favor; self-replicate on `spread` | the last star costs all of them (Law 19) | spent complete — they go into it and do not come back |
| universes | first install, silently, at 1 | hunger (the choice) | never spent | never — survives wipe of everything else; the cycle is canon |

The reassignment rule (Law 10) is kept: villagers idle as a currency for the
whole of act 1 before the deeper-offering ladder ambushes the player with the
bill — faith was priced in people all along, exactly as UP priced trust in
dollars. Wood and stone idle dead through act 2 and are reassigned one last
time as the ending's refund punchline.

What dies stranded, by design: wood and stone at molt 2 (mid-double-digits),
food at molt 3 (whatever the larder held), legend at molt 3 (typically 20–80),
doubt at molt 3 (uncounted), favor never — favor alone is balanced to zero,
because the ending is the one place tithe is exact where UP was exact.

## 3. the verb timeline

The whole game in verbs: gather → cut → assign → build → undertake → offer
(named) → miracle → tithe (the slider) → deepen → ascend → seed → tune → hold
the vigil → empty three skies → read → choose → quiet five organs → gather,
again, once per fleck. It opens and closes on the same verb (Law 24), and the
last gathering means the opposite of the first.

| verb | born | retired | retired by |
|---|---|---|---|
| gather berries (click) | t+0 | irrelevant by ~min 3 | foragers — the player was already the bot (Law 5) |
| cut wood (click) | ~t+15 s | molt 2 | wood's death |
| jobs +/- | first villager | molt 2 | purchase — the tithe's slider formalizes the rebalancing habit (Law 5) |
| buy works | <45 s | molt 2 | rows freeze; counts remain, costs vanish |
| undertakings | ~min 1 | the last star | the list ends as dialogue — cost column empties (§8) |
| an offering — name | ~5:30 | molt 2 | transmutes in place into the deeper offering (same button, doubling counts) |
| a deeper offering — N | molt 2 | 4th rung (8 villagers) | the ladder completing at faith 13 |
| miracles | turn 1 | ascension (act-2 set); dismantle (act-3 set) | the village their multipliers lived in is gone |
| the slider (fields—shrine) | molt 2 | dies in §8 step 3 | the one analog control; reborn relabeled at molt 3 (Law 18) |
| seed a herald | molt 3 | the last star | spent as fuel, all of them |
| the vigil (toggle) | the silence's arrival | §8 step 1 | let lapse, by hand |
| hunger / starve | one click | one click | the choice |
| gather berries, again | §8 | the Nth click, N = offerings | the debt running out |

Three disciplines carried over: verbs retire bottom-up (Law 4) — labor, then
logistics, then allocation, until the only job left is one slider and one
ladder; automation is sold only after the player has already become the bot
(Law 5) — the slider arrives after ~10 minutes of manual job-juggling toward
the same two ratios; and every automated chore hands its attention budget to a
new layer the same minute (Law 6) — the slider arrives in the same beat as
doubt, the thing the slider cannot manage.

The two rebirths (Law 18): the slider crosses molt 3 with its ends relabeled
(`fields — shrine` becomes `spread — reap`; same control, planetary scale),
and the first verb returns at the very end, transformed from foraging into
remembrance — one click per fleck, each click bearing a name.

## 4. the molts, frame by frame

UP demolishes; tithe transmutes. The law: same rows, same anchors, same field
— the next glance finds every piece in its place and the meaning changed.
Tithe spends wrongness where UP spent violence. No strobe exists anywhere in
this game; the loudest event (ascension) is the one with the most stillness
(Law 16). After each molt the dead economy is never mentioned again.

### molt 1 — the turn (shipped, unchanged)

First offering. LEX renames in place (actions→answers, the village→the flock,
villagers→stock, gather→grant); favor row wakes; sky stops warming, red creeps
(glow law inverted). v2 adds exactly one thing here: faith is born at 1,
displayed in the shrine section as `faith 1` — one of them believed first.

### molt 2 — the tithe (mid act 2)

Trigger: buying `the tithe` — the last bill the old economy ever pays,
audited in four currencies at once (Law 12): 250 favor + 60 food + 80 wood +
30 stone. On the same render:

- **stores (c1):** wood and stone rows blank — value and rate text empty, the
  slots remain (ghost law). Never mentioned again. food stays: stock must eat.
- **the flock (c2):** every jobs +/- button disables, permanently. The rows
  remain as readouts. The slider materializes in its reserved slot below the
  jobs table — ends labeled `fields` and `shrine` — and from this frame the
  job counts are a derivation of the slider, not an allocation. The rows you
  spent act 1 tending are now instruments reporting what the slider decides.
- **works (c2):** every building row freezes — cost text empties, count
  remains, the tease line stays in past tense. Nothing more is ever built by
  hand.
- **undertakings (c3):** the completed act-1 projects collapse to one summary
  line in their slot (sanctioned by DESIGN.md §3): `the early works — done`.
- **the hollow (c3):** the offer button transmutes in place: `an offering —
  wren` becomes `a deeper offering — 1` (§5). Same button, same anchor, new
  ladder. The surge line retires; the faith line takes its slot.
- **the field:** nothing changes. Same huts, same figures walking — but how
  many walk where now follows the slider. The same picture, suddenly a farm
  of people. That stillness is the molt.

### molt 3 — ascension (act 2 → act 3)

Trigger: buying `ascension — 13 faith + the flock + 2,000 favor`. The price
is the story (Law 19): every point of belief ever extended, every person, and
the bank. On the same render:

- **the flock (c2):** the population line transmutes to `the first village:
  tithed` for sixty seconds, then to `worlds 0 / 14 · souls 0`. Jobs rows
  blank (slots remain). The slider's ends relabel: `spread` — `reap`.
- **stores (c1):** food row blanks. souls row wakes (it has sat as a ghost
  since boot, the row that exists before it has ever been nonzero —
  precision as dread). favor remains: the wallet crosses.
- **works (c2):** building rows blank. One new row appears in the list, in
  unlock order: `a herald — 400 favor`.
- **undertakings (c3):** act-2 completed projects collapse to `the
  congregation — done`. miracles list (c1) blanks; act-3 miracles arrive in
  the same slots.
- **the field:** the figures are gone. Next frame, no transition. The huts
  stand dark (build color drops to its unlit value), the fire is out, smoke
  stops, the temple alone keeps its color. The flecks remain. And the stars —
  fourteen of them, drawn from the seed since the first boot — are now the
  board. The field was always showing you act 3.

No fanfare at any molt: no banner, no line of prose, zero added text. Molt 2
gets one button relabel; molt 3 gets one population-line stamp. The biggest
events in the game are the quietest (Law 16).

## 5. the spine — faith

UP's spine is trust: awarded by unseen parents on a Fibonacci ladder, spent
whole at the climax. Tithe's spine is **faith** — the flock's regard for the
god. Lowercase, integer, born at 1 at turn 1 (one of them believed first).
Nobody unseen awards it: the flock does, and the metric is what they have
already given. Favor is extracted belief, so cumulative favor IS the
measurement — the more taken, the more believed. That circle is the act.

**The schedule.** Thirteen points total, on two ladders that must both
complete (Law 12 — the door audits every wallet):

- **eight gates** on cumulative favor (totalFavor), Fibonacci-spaced ×~1.65:
  `25, 65, 130, 235, 405, 680, 1125, 2900`. Silent: the faith number changes,
  nothing else (Law 16). The last gate breaks the ratio (×2.6) on purpose: it
  sits past what the act-2 caps can grant by parking alone, so it opens only
  when a sign bought at the cap refills — the sign ladder pumps the gate
  ladder, and the race's two exponentials (§6.5) touch through one wallet.
  (Tuned from 1850 in M5: at 1850 the gate fell ~60 s after the molt
  unconditionally and the §6.5 stagger was unreachable.)
- **four deeper offerings** — the Tokens-of-Goodwill move. After molt 2 the
  offer button's ladder reads `a deeper offering — 1`, then `— 2`, `— 4`,
  `— 8`: villagers, at doubling counts, +1 faith each. Faith was priced in
  people from the start; the doubling makes the endgame a herd-savings race.
  The 8-rung requires a full village (pop cap 10) and guts it.
- starting balance 1. Total: 1 + 8 + 4 = **13** — a Fibonacci member, and the
  exact price of ascension. Faith dies complete at 13/13, the only spine ever
  fully spent, because its thirteenth point was a key, not a balance.

**What faith gates** (no allocation sub-game — UP's processor/memory split
does not translate; tithe's allocation drama lives in the slider instead):

- priest headcount cap = current faith (the flock gives only as many voices
  as it believes). Migration clamps shipped saves (§6.6).
- favor cap = `200 × faith` (so the 2,000-favor ascension bank fits only
  under late-ladder caps — the cap is itself a forecast, Law 3).
- miracle reveals: each miracle requires a faith floor (§6.3).
- display: `faith 1` in the shrine section from turn 1; becomes `faith N / 13`
  the moment `other lights` is bought — the ceiling shown long before it is
  reachable is the standing threat (Law 2).

**Legend — the overflow currency (Law 7).** While favor sits at its cap,
legend accrues at 0.3/s. Spending favor drops it below cap, so worship and
myth-making are mutually exclusive: the god must sit still to be storied.
Legend buys the act's three quiet projects (§6.4) and dies stranded at molt 3.

**Doubt — the anti-currency.** Born the moment the first deeper offering is
taken: +1 doubt per rung, +1 per 90 s while any doubt remains (heresy
compounds). A doubter neither works nor worships; on the field the figure
parks at the treeline edge, facing away (anchor col 10, row 10.2 + index
offset — reduced motion identical). Threshold, not gradient (Law 15): below
`doubt >= stock/2` doubt is only a tax; at it, the tithe halts entirely —
favor rate reads 0.0 until cured. Stall, never loss: nobody leaves, nothing
is destroyed, the readout just stops. The cure is `a sign` (§6.3), priced on
its own doubling-ish ladder, and the tension between buying signs and banking
for ascension is the act's cliffhanger machinery (§6.5).

OWNER CALL: no combat anywhere in tithe — UP's war translates to the silence
(act 3) and doubt (act 2), both threshold-stalls. Alternative: DESIGN.md §3's
conquest hosts with win-rolls; it costs the tone more than it pays.

## 6. act 2 in full — the congregation

Target span 15–60 min attended. The act's shape: worship economy (faith gates
1–4) → the tithe, molt 2 (~minute 25–30) → herd management under the slider
(gates 5–8, deeper offerings) → the ascension race. The optimization problem:
birth rate vs tithe rate vs doubt, all pulling on one slider and one ladder.

### 6.1 jobs (additions to JOBS)

| id | name | base | out | eats | show |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| c | cultivator | — | arrivals: threshold ×0.85 per | 1 | `s.mir.goodyear` |

Priest (shipped) gains the faith cap: max priests = faith. After molt 2 all
job rows become slider-derived readouts: fields hands = `round((1−u)·stock)`
split foragers/cultivators by need; shrine hands = the rest, priests first
(to the faith cap), remainder worship as congregation at half priest rate.
The +/- buttons disable forever; the rows stay (the ledger may never outgrow
the picture — and the picture never lies about who walks where).

New site: cultivators walk to the farms (SITES.c = col 27, row 10.6).

### 6.3 miracles (additions to MIR; favor-priced)

The ascension row sits visible and unaffordable for most of the act — the
HypnoDrones teaser (Law 2). Its cost line reads exactly:
`13 faith + the flock + 2,000 favor`. The middle term is the
characterization (Law 19); "the flock" means every remaining villager,
converted at nothing — they are not exchanged, they are spent.

OWNER CALL: ascension is a single one-way door, not DESIGN.md §3's repeatable
divinity prestige (divinity ≥ 3 forced loop). A 90-minute evening cannot
afford replaying acts 1–2 three times; UP never resets mid-run, and the
forced-repeat pattern is Kittens, not Paperclips. Alternative: keep the
divinity loop and accept a 3–4 h first sitting.

### 6.5 the ascension race (Law 20, placed deliberately)

Two exponentials crossing a deadline: the sign ladder (220 × 1.5^n, the cost
of keeping worship alive) versus the favor bank (2,000 under a cap that only
the faith ladder raises), while doubt compounds (+1/90 s) and each deeper
offering cuts the workforce that earns the bank. Most runs arrive at faith
12 with the bank one sign-purchase short — the wall lands one decision past
the player's reach, and nobody authored which one. Pace-sim must verify: the
final stretch holds three live timers (gate 8, the 8-rung herd rebuild, the
bank) staggered 30–90 s apart (Law 8), and no flat stretch exceeds 3 min.

The one unprompted aside in the whole game (Law 17) fires here: the first
time the tithe's favor rate exceeds the food rate, the shrine tease shows,
once, for 60 s, never again: `they do not count themselves.`

### 6.6 changes to shipped content

1. **favor gains a cap** (`200 × faith`) — required for legend (Law 7).
   SAVE_VER 3 → 4; migration clamps favor to cap.
2. **priest cap = faith** — migration clamps `jobs.p` to faith (1 for any
   pre-v4 turn-1 save).
3. **`the tithe` gets its real cost** (was `cost:null`, "soon").
4. **the offer button gains its second life** post-molt-2 (deeper offerings).
   `OFFER` yield ladder unchanged before molt 2.
5. **new boot-time ghosts** (HTML additions, no layout shift ever): job row
   `cultivator`, the slider slot under the jobs table, store rows `legend`
   and `souls`, the faith line in the shrine section, `universe N` text slot
   in the top meta strip.
6. **state additions**: `faith, legend, doubt, slider, heralds, worlds,
   souls, galaxy, vigil, universes, endState` + reveal keys. `universes`
   defaults to 1 and is excluded from wipe.

## 7. act 3 in full — the hunger

Target span 60–85 min attended. No new screen, no new anchors: the board is
the fourteen seed-drawn stars the field has shown since first boot. A tithed
world's star turns favor-red `#a14034`; a world the silence takes goes faint;
the sky empties three times. The dead village stays in the picture, dark,
under the whole act — the field is the log, and the log now reads in lights.

### 7.1 the board

- **worlds**: 14 per sky (galaxy), converted serially in star order. World i
  (0-based) requires `40 × 1.6^i` herald-seconds of `spread`. A world the
  silence has dimmed costs ×2 (stall, not loss — it can always be relit).
- **souls**: on conversion, `souls += 1e6 × 3^i × 1000^g` (g = sky index
  0–2). Sky totals ≈ 2.4e12 / 2.4e15 / 2.4e18. Display: spelled suffixes
  (extend `fmt` through b, t, qa, qi); with `the count` bought, one decimal.
  OWNER CALL: total ~2.4e18 (alt: DESIGN.md §3's 1e60–80 with five+ skies —
  louder, longer, still plain-JS-safe; default is the quieter scale because
  tithe's numbers law is smallness).
- **favor flow**: each tithed world pays `0.8 × 1000^g × reap` favor/s
  (reap = slider right share). Favor cap is gone after ascension — the flock
  that set it is gone; legend never accrues again.

### 7.2 heralds

| def | value |
|---|---|
| seed cost | `400 favor × 1.25^owned` (row in the works list: `a herald`) |
| flavor (one-time) | it walks until it is heard. |
| replication | `dh/dt = 0.02 × heralds × spread` — doubling ≈ 35 s at full spread |
| cap | 144 (a Fibonacci square; the row reads `heralds 144` and the cost text empties) |
| field | red figures on the ridgeline, row 5.6, cols 20–90, max 7 drawn |
| refund | `recall the heralds` — 100% of seed cost, any time (Law 14): act 3's experiments must be free |

The slider (relabeled `spread — reap` at molt 3) is the whole allocation
game: spread converts and replicates, reap pays favor. Parked at full spread
the treasury starves and the vigil lapses; parked at full reap the board
stalls. Capital is loyal to the slider — heralds keep existing through any
setting, but the attention dial can idle the entire conversion economy.

### 7.3 the silence

The rival nothing-god, translated from UP's drifters as a threshold-stall
(Law 15), arriving exactly like the war did — at a precise count:

- **born** at the 5th converted world, honest to the digit. One unconverted
  light goes faint between two renders. No text.
- **pressure**: while unheld, the silence dims one unconverted light per
  40 s. It never touches a tithed world — what is given is kept.
- **the vigil**: a toggle row in the works list, `the vigil — 8 favor/s ×
  1000^g`. While paid, zero losses; unpaid, the dimming runs. Below the
  line you stall (each world you relight, it dims another — net zero);
  above it you cannot lose. The drama is affording the line.
- act-3 miracles (the mirList slots, repopulated):

| id | name | flavor (one-time) | cost | effect | show |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| tongues | tongues | every door opens from inside. | 1,200 favor | heralds convert ×2 | sky 1 ≥ 8 worlds |
| hush | the hush | it has nowhere left to stand. | 2,500 favor × 1000^g | the vigil costs nothing, this sky | the silence active 90 s |

### 7.4 the sky doors and the last star

| id | name | flavor (one-time) | cost | effect | show |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| sky2 | another sky | the dark is not empty yet. | 2.0e12 souls | re-roll: 14 stars relight, dimmer; world costs ×1; souls ×1000 | sky 1 at 14/14 |
| sky3 | another sky | (same row, mutated price) | 2.0e15 souls | as above | sky 2 at 14/14 |
| last | the last star | — (no flavor; the name is enough) | all the heralds | consume world 42; every rate reads 0.0 — the first zero that will never move | sky 3 at 13/14 |

The doors are billed in souls — the tithe spends the tithed (Law 12: each
door in a different currency; favor bought the heralds, souls buy the skies,
the heralds themselves buy the end). `the last star` sits revealed at 13/14
with its price visible: the player's replicating children, all of them, go
into it and do not come back (Law 19 — billed in the one thing the player
believed was theirs).

After it: souls frozen at the final total, favor frozen, the field's sky
black except the flecks' red and one dark village. Clips-per-second moment:
the souls rate line reads `0.0/s` and never moves again.

## 8. the ending, beat by beat

### the choice

Three cost-less rows appear in the undertakings list, one at a time, 8 s
apart — the moment the price column is empty, the economy is over and
someone is speaking (interface doc; Emperor-of-Drift device):

1. `the silence` — tease: `it was here before the first light.`
2. `the count` — tease: `you have done this before.` — and the top meta
   strip wakes its ghost slot: `universe 1` (tracked since first install;
   Law 21 — the game's one acknowledgment that a person was present).
3. `the offer` — tease: `it can begin again. or it can stop.`

Then two adjacent rows:

- `hunger` — tease: `begin again. the village will not remember. you will.`
  Effect: universes +1; full reset to fresh state with a new seed; all
  production ×1.5 per completed universe; the names reshuffle; the flecks
  belong to the next village. The cycle is canon — this is turn 2's reveal,
  not a meta-mechanic.
- `starve` — tease: `refuse.`

### starve — the five quietings

Each a cost-less row (the economy is over), arriving as the previous one is
clicked. Each return wakes a dead store row with a single digit — the refund
punchline (Law 23): after e18 souls, three wood is the most dramatic
quantity in the game.

| step | row | what happens | what returns |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `let the vigil lapse` | the vigil row greys; nothing dims — there is nothing left to take | nothing |
| 2 | `unmake the miracles` | mirList empties to ghosts; every multiplier dies | food row wakes: `2.0` |

## 9. the laws applied

The UP laws used, each in its tithe form (numbers from PAPERCLIPS-DESIGN §8):

- **Law 2** — the ascension row and `faith N / 13` sit visible and
  unaffordable for half of act 2; `the last star` shows its price at 13/14.
- **Law 3** — the menu teaches by pricing: favor caps (`200 × faith`) are
  set one ladder-rung above each act-2 goal; sky doors price themselves at
  roughly one sky's souls.
- **Law 4** — verbs retire bottom-up: clicks, then wood, then jobs, then
  building, until only the slider and the ladder remain.
- **Law 5** — the slider is sold only after ~10 min of manual job-juggling;
  it changes who performs the allocation, never what happens.
- **Law 6** — the slider arrives in the same beat as doubt; automation hands
  its attention budget to heresy.
- **Law 7** — legend accrues only while favor sits at cap; stillness is the
  only way to be storied.
- **Law 8** — three timers staggered 30–90 s everywhere: gates, arrivals,
  deeper offerings (act 2); replication, vigil, doors (act 3).
- **Law 9** — molt, never inflate — transmuted: same rows, same anchors,
  meaning replaced in one render; the dead economy never mentioned again.
- **Law 10** — villagers idle as a wallet all of act 1 before the deeper
  offerings present the bill; wood and stone wait dead until the refunds.
- **Law 11** — wood, stone, food, legend, doubt all die stranded, unmourned.
- **Law 12** — ascension audits faith + flock + favor at once; the three
  endgame doors bill favor, souls, heralds — every wallet, in turn.
- **Law 13** — every miracle moves the bottleneck: obedience → arrivals →
  food → the cap.
- **Law 14** — `recall the heralds` refunds 100%; doubt and the silence
  stall, never destroy.
- **Law 15** — doubt at stock/2 halts the tithe entirely; the silence is
  held wholly or not at all. No gradients, no combat.
- **Law 16** — fanfare inversely scales: faith gates get a digit, molt 3
  gets stillness, the silence arrives as one dim light; only turn 1 keeps
  its one sentence.
- **Law 17** — one aside, ever: `they do not count themselves.`
- **Law 18** — the slider crosses molt 3 relabeled; the first verb returns
  at the end transformed into remembrance.
- **Law 19** — prices characterize: `the flock` as a cost term; the last
  star billed in heralds; the climax always billed in what felt permanent.
- **Law 20** — the ascension race: sign ladder vs favor bank vs doubt, the
  wall landing one decision past reach, unauthored.
- **Law 21** — `universe 1` and the click-per-offering ending: the only
  acknowledgments a person was present.
- **Law 22** — one word carries the flock: the population line's mood walks
  `content → quiet → watchful → waiting → given`.
- **Law 23** — the end balances to the decimal: the shrine billed at exactly
  the remaining favor; exactly one click per offering.
- **Law 24** — first verb = last verb: gather berries, meaning hope, then
  meaning debt.
